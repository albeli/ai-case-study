# PolyAI: Revolutionizing Conversational AI in Customer Service

## Overview and Origin

- **Name of company:** PolyAI
- **Incorporation Date of the Company:** 2017
- **Founders of the company** Nikola Mrkšić, Tsung-Hsien Wen, and Pei-Hao Su
- **Origin of the Company or Project Idea** The founders met at Cambridge's dialog systems group and wanted to solve the complex problem of enabling natural, helpful conversations between humans and robots.
- **Funding and Financial Backing** PolyAI recently raised $40 million in a Series B funding round, bringing their total funding to $70 million. This round was led by Georgian, with participation from Twilio Ventures, Khosla Ventures, Point72 Ventures, and Amadeus Capital Partners.

## Business Activities

- **Problem Addressed by the Company or Project** PolyAI aims to transform traditional customer service call centers with AI, enabling natural and efficient voice interactions.
- **Intended Customer Base and Market Size** Enterprises seeking to enhance customer service experience.
- **Unique Solutions and Competitive Advantages** PolyAI offers a voice-first conversational AI platform, capable of handling complex inquiries in a natural, human-like manner. The company's focus is on developing voice assistants that understand natural speech, regardless of accent or background noise, and can adapt to changes in the conversation. This emphasizes their commitment to creating a more human-like conversational AI.
- **Technologies in Use and Their Implementation** PolyAI uses generative AI, proprietary NLU, real-time SLU, United ASR, and natural speech synthesis, creating a seamless conversational experience.
- **Business Impact and Adoption:** PolyAI's voice assistants are being used by notable companies such as FedEx, Marriott Hotels, and Whitbread PLC. This indicates a significant level of adoption and trust in PolyAI's technology in the market.

## Landscape

- **Company's Field of Operation** Operating in the dynamic field of Conversational AI, PolyAI specializes in voice AI technology, revolutionizing customer service. At its core, PolyAI focuses on crafting advanced voice assistants for call centers, designed not just to respond but to understand—mirroring natural human conversation. These AI-powered assistants excel in handling customer inquiries with unprecedented efficiency, thereby elevating customer experience and streamlining business operations. A standout feature of PolyAI’s technology is its sophisticated processing of natural speech, adept at navigating a myriad of accents and dialects. This capability ensures personalized and inclusive customer interactions. By consistently pushing the boundaries in conversational AI, PolyAI emerges as a trailblazer, adeptly responding to and shaping the future of customer service technology.
- **Major Trends and Innovations in the Last 5-10 Years** Over the last 5 to 10 years, the field of conversational AI, particularly in the realm of voice AI for customer service, has undergone remarkable transformations. These advancements encompass a wide range of areas, from enhanced natural language processing and understanding to sophisticated emotion recognition. The following list highlights key trends that are shaping the future of customer interactions through AI:

- **Advancements in NLP and NLU**: Major strides have been made in AI's ability to process and understand human language, resulting in better handling of dialects, accents, and colloquialisms. This has led to a significant improvement in AI’s contextual understanding.

- **Improved Speech Recognition**: Today’s voice AI technologies are far more proficient in accurately transcribing spoken words, an invaluable asset in diverse acoustic settings, and for handling a range of accents and speech patterns.

- **Generative AI for Conversational Agents**: The use of generative AI models has enabled AI systems to conduct more fluid, dynamic, and context-aware conversations, bringing a new level of sophistication to customer interactions.

- **Integration with Other Technologies**: The integration of voice AI with systems like CRM software has led to more holistic and efficient customer service solutions, facilitating a seamless experience.

- **Focus on Personalization**: There’s been a shift towards delivering personalized customer experiences, with AI systems now using data analytics to tailor interactions according to individual preferences and history.

- **Emotion Recognition and Sentiment Analysis**: Some of the latest systems include the ability to detect and respond to the emotional tone of customers, greatly enhancing the quality and effectiveness of interactions.

- **Multi-Modal Systems**: The development and deployment of systems that combine voice with other modalities, such as text and visual cues, have enriched the user interaction experience.

- **Wider Application Across Industries**: Voice AI is now being adopted in various sectors, from healthcare to retail, demonstrating its versatility in applications ranging from customer support to transaction processing.

- **Voice Bots in Omni-Channel Support**: As a key component in omni-channel strategies, voice AI offers consistent and efficient service across multiple channels, including phone, web, and mobile.

- **Advancements in Security and Privacy**: With voice AI systems often handling sensitive data, significant progress has been made in ensuring data privacy and security, notably through enhanced encryption and adherence to regulations like GDPR.

These trends not only underscore the rapid advancement of voice AI technologies but also indicate a future where AI-driven customer service becomes increasingly integrated into everyday business operations.

- **Key Competitors in the Field** In the field of conversational AI and voice AI technology, several major companies are noteworthy for their contributions and innovations. Some of these include:

1. **Google:** Through products like Google Assistant and Dialogflow, Google is a key player in voice recognition and conversational AI.

2. **Amazon:** Known for its Alexa voice service, Amazon has significantly influenced the development of voice AI, especially in consumer markets.

3. **Apple:** With Siri, Apple has been a pioneer in integrating voice AI into mobile devices and everyday technology.

4. **Microsoft:** Microsoft's Cortana and its Azure Bot Service provide robust AI capabilities, particularly for enterprise applications.

5. **IBM:** IBM Watson offers a suite of enterprise-grade AI services, including conversation AI and language processing tools.

6. **Nuance Communications:** A leader in speech recognition, Nuance offers specialized AI solutions for healthcare, automotive, and financial services.

7. **SoundHound Inc.:** Known for its Houndify platform, SoundHound provides voice-enabled AI and conversational intelligence solutions.

8. **OpenAI:** While not exclusively focused on voice AI, OpenAI's advancements in language models like GPT-3 have implications for conversational AI.

9. **Rasa:** Rasa offers open-source tools for building conversational AI, with a focus on customizable solutions for businesses.

10. **DialogTech (formerly Ifbyphone):** Specializes in AI-driven voice analytics and automation solutions for marketing and contact centers.

Each of these companies contributes uniquely to the field, either through direct consumer interaction AI, like Siri and Alexa, or through enterprise-level solutions that power customer service and other applications. They are driving innovation in natural language processing, machine learning, and the integration of AI into diverse areas of technology and daily life.

##  Insights from CEO Nikola Mrkšić

On the podcast, Nikola Mrkšić, CEO of PolyAI, discussed their unique approach to voice technology. Here are the key details from that part of the conversation:

- **Crafting Unique Voices for Each Client:** Mrkšić emphasized PolyAI’s distinctive strategy in creating a new voice for every client. This approach is not about using generic or standard voices but crafting unique ones that reflect each client's specific needs and brand identity.

- **Diversity in Voice AI:** He mentioned the extreme diversity of their client base and how PolyAI caters to a wide range of requirements with very different voice characteristics. This diversity extends to different tones, emotions, sentiments, speech styles, and even slang, tailoring the voice AI to suit each client’s unique profile.

- **Importance of User Experience in Voice Technology:** Mrkšić stressed the importance of the user experience when interacting with voice assistants. He suggested that the current voice AI offerings in the market only scratch the surface of potential user experiences. PolyAI aims to delve deeper, creating voices that resonate more authentically with users.

- **Customization and Emotional Connection:** The focus on customization in voice AI underscores the importance of emotional connection and relatability in automated interactions. PolyAI’s approach indicates a shift from generic voice solutions to more personalized, engaging experiences that better serve the clients and their customers' needs.

These insights demonstrate PolyAI's commitment to advancing voice AI technology by focusing on customization, diversity, and a deeper understanding of user experience. This approach sets them apart in the field of conversational AI, aiming to provide more natural and engaging voice interactions.

## Client Testimonial and Use Case

### Landry's
Brian Jeppesen's experience at Landry’s demonstrates the practical applications and benefits of PolyAI's technology. This real-world example provides insight into how businesses can leverage AI for customer service.

- **Impact on Customer Service Efficiency:** Highlighting Landry's challenges during COVID-19, such as difficulties in hiring and high abandon rates, and how PolyAI's solution effectively addressed these issues.

- **Human-like Interaction Quality:** Emphasizing the quality of PolyAI’s voice AI, which was designed to sound like Landry’s best agents, thus avoiding the typical robotic interactions of automated systems.

- **Call Containment Success:** Including the impressive statistic of over 80% call containment rate achieved from day one, significantly higher than the anticipated 40-50%.

- **Volume Handling and Performance Metrics:** Mentioning the specific volume of calls handled by the voice assistant, such as 40,000 more calls for one property and handling 3,000 reservations in a month at a hotel.

- **Operational Benefits:** The testimonial touches on the operational efficiency and reliability of the voice assistant, which can work continuously without the challenges faced by human agents.

- **Advice for AI Implementation:** Brian’s advice for businesses considering call center voice AI, such as starting with basic tasks and FAQs and focusing on low-hanging fruits, is valuable for understanding the strategic deployment of this technology.


## Results

- **Business Impact of the Company** PolyAI has significantly impacted the conversational AI landscape, particularly in customer service, by offering innovative voice AI solutions that have been widely adopted by various high-profile clients. The company's technology has led to notable improvements in operational efficiency for these clients, including better handling of call volumes and enhanced customer service experiences. This is evidenced by PolyAI's achievement of a 50% call handling rate and an impressive 85% Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) score in hotel guest interactions, demonstrating the effectiveness and reliability of their AI systems. Additionally, the implementation of PolyAI's solutions has enabled businesses to capture more sales opportunities and differentiate themselves competitively in the market. The company's growth, underscored by significant funding rounds and its contribution to the broader field of AI, highlights its success and the potential for continued impact in the industry.
- **Core Success Metrics and Company Performance** In the field of conversational AI, particularly for companies like PolyAI that specialize in voice AI for customer service, several core metrics are crucial for measuring success. The call handling rate is a primary metric, indicating the efficiency and capacity of AI systems in managing customer calls. A high rate signifies the system's ability to effectively handle a large volume of inquiries, thereby reducing the burden on human agents and improving operational efficiency. Another vital metric is the Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) score, which reflects the quality of customer interactions and overall satisfaction with the service provided. This score is particularly important in industries where customer experience directly impacts brand perception and loyalty. Additionally, revenue generation is a critical measure, as the implementation of conversational AI solutions often aims to increase sales conversions and overall business revenue. Lastly, customer retention is a key indicator, as successful AI interactions can lead to higher customer loyalty and repeat business, essential for long-term growth and sustainability. Together, these metrics provide a comprehensive view of a conversational AI company's performance, effectiveness, and impact on client businesses.
- **Growth and Expansion:** PolyAI has experienced over 5x growth this year alone, now employing over 100 people. They have expanded with offices in New York and California, in addition to their London base, showing significant geographical and market expansion.
- **Future Direction:** The company aims to revolutionize customer service lines with their voice assistants, moving away from traditional IVR systems towards more natural and efficient voice interactions.

4. **Chatbot to Voice AI Transition Services:** Develop a seamless transition service from chatbots to voice AI. This would cater to businesses looking to upgrade their existing text-based AI services to more advanced voice interactions.

5. **AI-Powered Marketing Tools:** Create marketing-focused AI tools that can conduct market research, analyze trends, and even manage or suggest content for marketing campaigns.

6. **Customizable AI Personalities:** Offer services that allow businesses to customize the personality of their AI voice assistants, aligning with their brand tone and customer expectations.

7. **Integration Services with Existing Infrastructure:** Provide comprehensive integration services for businesses to seamlessly incorporate PolyAI’s technology into their existing IT and customer service infrastructure.

8. **Training and Consultation Services:** Offer training and consultation for businesses to maximize using PolyAI’s technology. This could include best practices for AI interaction design and data management.

9. **Accessibility Features:** Develop additional features to make voice AI more accessible to users with disabilities, ensuring inclusivity in customer service.

10. **Voice AI for Internal Business Processes:** Expand the application of voice AI to internal business processes such as HR and internal helpdesks, optimizing efficiency within organizations.

By diversifying and expanding its product and service offerings in these areas, PolyAI could address a wider range of customer needs and tap into new market segments, further establishing its position as a leader in the conversational AI field.
